FAQs

Be prepared to enter campus with these items before the candidate lineup starts on upper campus at 8:00 a.m. for the Celebration for the Classes of 2020 and 2021. Make sure to inform your guests about what they should bring with them on Commencement day.

  • Cap 
  • Ticket
  • Proof of Vaccination, or Negative COVID-19 Test (time-stamped photo of an at-home antigen test on the day of each event or email results of a PCR test within 48 hours of the first attended event) 
  • Mask
  • Water Bottle
  • Prepare for inclement weather
  • Feeling sick? Stay home and tune in virtually.

2020 and 2021 graduates may attend these celebrations and activities. Each graduate is allowed to bring 2 (two) guests.

Registration is required to participate in these make-up activities and will open in April. Be on the lookout for a communication from your school with more details and update your information in the Columbia Alumni Association directory to stay informed.

Full regalia is optional for the Classes of 2020 and 2021 but caps will be required. Regalia is available from the Columbia University Bookstore or online. 

  • Bachelors and Masters Degrees: Orders can be placed in-store starting March 8. Online orders may be placed beginning March 14 and ending April 27 to be directly shipped to the student by visiting columbia20202021.shopoakhalli.com.
     
  • Doctoral Degrees: Head wear may be reserved in store from March 8 through April 11th without incurring a late fee. In-store pickup and ship-to-home options are available. Online orders may be placed beginning March 14 and ending April 27 by visiting columbia20202021.shopoakhalli.com. Head wear and accessories ordered will be shipped directly to your home. 

Please reach out to your individual school to receive any documentation related to obtaining visas.

We welcome alumni to volunteer as Event Hosts for ceremonies held on the Morningside Campus. Event Hosts provide welcoming support and guidance to graduates and their guests attending events on campus from Friday, March 13, through Wednesday, May 18.

For more information, please email [email protected]

Those requiring accommodations during the Classes of 2020 and 2021 Celebration and corresponding events, including alumni and guests whose disabilities limit their ability to walk or negotiate stairs, should contact Disability Services at [email protected] by Wednesday, May 4. To learn more visit Disability Services. 

Below is the timeline for this event.

7:30 a.m. Gates open for General Admission Outdoor seating: Line forms on Broadway at 115th Street to access General Admission gates Disability access: For individuals with disabilities or limited mobility, and those needing assistance, line forms at 115th and 116th Streets and Amsterdam Avenue
8:00 a.m. Graduate lineup begins
8:30 a.m. Graduate procession begins
9:00 a.m. Celebration for the Classes of 2020 and 2021 begins
