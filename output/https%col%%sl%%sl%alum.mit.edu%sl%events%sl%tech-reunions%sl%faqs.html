Frequently Asked Questions

To prepare you for your trip back to campus we are providing a list of the most common Tech Reunions questions (below).

For additional inquiries email reunions@mit.edu or call 617-253-8824.

About Tech Reunions

What is the Code of Conduct for Tech Reunions?

The Institute promotes the principle that every person brings unique qualities and talents to the community and that every individual should be treated in a respectful manner. All members of the MIT community are expected to conduct themselves with professionalism, personal integrity, and respect for the rights, differences and dignity of others. These standards of personal conduct apply to all communications, whether oral, written, or in gestures. Community members are also expected to treat the property of both the Institute and other community members with appropriate care and respect. 

What is MIT Tech Reunions?

Tech Reunions is a multiday event, presented by the Alumni Association, that honors class milestones and celebrates the MIT experience. The schedule features only-at-MIT traditions held on campus, with some events also available online.

When is MIT Tech Reunions?

MIT Tech Reunions will take place in person from Friday, May 27, to Sunday, May 29. The 50th reunion activities will begin on Thursday, May 26, and end on Sunday, May 29.

Where do I pick up my reunion materials or register for events on campus?

Is there shuttle service around campus?

Shuttles will be available to all alumni and guests. Shuttles will stop at the Hyatt Cambridge and Marriott Kendall hotels, residence halls, parking lots, and several other venues on campus. Learn more here.

In addition to the Tech Reunions shuttles, reunion attendees may ride the MIT Tech Shuttle. It runs MondayFriday, 6:15 a.m.7:10 p.m. 

How do I park on campus?

Parking passes for campus lots are currently sold out. Please utilize public transportation or street parking. If you purchased a parking pass, you should have received it in the mail along with a map on where you can park on which days.

Will I need an alumni ID to enter campus buildings?

Alumni ID or Tim Tickets will not be required for building access. A map of building access and times will be available soon.

Can I bring my children to Tech Reunions?

There will be many opportunities for family friendly events throughout the weekend. This includes the Family Maker Fair and Tech Challenge Games. Attendees should make sure to organize for any necessary childcare if attending events without their children. 

Can I attend Commencement?

Undergraduate and graduate alumni from the 50th reunion classes of 1970, 1971, and 1972 are invited to march in the Commencement procession. To participate, 50th reunion  alumni can register for the Commencement exercises event on their reunions registration form. Each 50th reunion alumnus/a may invite one guest, who can view the alumni procession from a VIP tent on Memorial Drive and watch the exercises on television in nearby air-conditioned Killian Hall. 

The 25th reunion classes of 1995, 1996, and 1997 are invited to attend Commencement but do not march.  25th reunion alumni can register to attend Commencement on their reunion registration form. Due to limited space, guests of 25th reunion alumni are not able to attend Commencement. 

All other alumni and guests can watch a live video broadcast of the ceremony in viewing centers across campus.Visit the Commencement website for more details.

How can I locate a classmate?

You can use the MIT Online Alumni Directory to locate friends and classmates. You can search by class year, course, and more. While you're logged in, you can check and update your own personal information as well. 

What is the dress code?

The dress code will vary for each event. Check your class schedule and/or the Tech Reunions schedule for details. 

Where can I find my Reunion Class Book?

Visit the Reunion Class Book FAQ page to learn how to create, edit, and read your class book. 

How is Tech Reunions supporting sustainability?

The MIT Alumni Association is proud to support the MIT Energy Initiative by minimizing the energy footprint of Tech Reunions. We are happy to offer online registration, electronic confirmations, a digital program book and app, and reusable parking cards. 

Where is the lost and found located?

Lost and found during Tech Reunions will be held at the Registration and Check-in area during registration hours. After registration closes, all items will be given to MIT Police. 

Where is MIT Medical located?

MIT Medical is located at 25 Carleton St., Bldg. E23, between Amherst St. and Main St., and is open to alumni and families who require medical attention during Tech Reunions. Medical personnel are available daily from 7:00 a.m.11:00 p.m., and medical staff is available by phone, 24 hours a day, at 617-253-4481. For on-campus emergencies, dial 100 on any campus phone. Please be aware that you will be billed for any medical services rendered. 

What happens if I have an emergency?

Police, fire, and medical emergencies should be directed to the MIT Campus Police. Dial 100 on any campus phone for any on-campus emergency.

What are our COVID Protocols for Tech Reunion?

While cases on MITs campus are currently decreasing, they are rising across the country and in Massachusetts. For these reasons, we strongly encourage graduates and guests to take precautions before Commencement ceremonies and Tech Reunions. 

Tech Reunions will adhere to the most current MIT Covid-19 policies that can be found on the MIT Now website. Please note: 

  • Masks are required on MIT shuttles. 
  • High-quality masks (N95, KN95, or KF94) are strongly recommended in indoor spaces and will be provided. 

To help stop the spread of Covid-19, everyone can test before traveling and they can wear a high-quality mask while traveling and/or indoors. Consistent with these safeguards, MIT is strongly encouraging all MIT graduates and their guestsas well as participants, staff, and volunteersto wear high-quality masks during indoor Commencement ceremonies and Tech Reunions events. The Institute will make face coverings available for free at all Commencement events. 

The Institute expects that individuals attending Commencement and Tech Reunions who are eligible to be vaccinated will be up-to-date on their Covid vaccinations or have a medical or religious reason they cannot receive the vaccine. We strongly encourage attendees who are unvaccinated or who have concerns to wear a well-fitting mask at all Commencement and Tech Reunion events. 

What If I Become Symptomatic or Test Positive While on Campus?

Please report immediately to Tech Reunions organizers, reunions@mit.edu, if you become symptomatic or test positive while on campus and acknowledge that you/your guests will need to leave campus directly and are responsible for securing isolation accommodations.

Hotels & Residence Halls

Are MIT hotel blocks available?

MIT hotel room blocks are currently sold out. For a full list of area hotels, please visit Hotels and Accommodations.

Are residence halls available to book during Tech Reunions?

Residence halls are sold out and are not available for purchase at on-site registration.

 

Can you help me with special accommodations?

Special accommodations are available. Please contact the Tech Reunions staff for more information. Attendees can also specify their requirements during the registration process.

Can I access the Internet if I am staying in a residence hall?

Residence hall rooms and other locations on campus have wireless access for use by alumni and guests. It is easy to connect to the MIT GUEST wireless network; your device should be able to "see" the network as one of the open wireless options and connect instantly.

What are the residence hall policies?

All guests must abide by the Tech Reunions Housing Codes of Conduct, detailed below. 

COVID Policy:

If you become symptomatic or test positive while on campus, please report immediately to Tech Reunion organizers, reunions@mit.edu, and acknowledge that you/your guests will need to leave campus directly and will be responsible for securing isolation accommodations.

 

Additional Other information:

  • All alumni and guests will need to show a photo ID when entering a residence hall.
  • Name badges must be worn to enter the building and in all of the building's public areas.
  • Room assignment are done just a few days before reunions, and they do their best to accommodate specific room requests.
  • Check-in is at 3:00 p.m., but rooms may be available earlier. Check-out is 11:00 a.m.
  • Most bathrooms are all-gender, and there are no private bathrooms. Bring a bathrobe and slippers. Students will also be utilizing these facilities.
  • App accessed laundry facilities are available.
  • Residence halls are not air-conditioned.
  • Seniors and other students will be living in the residence halls during reunions.
  • Residence hall rooms and other locations on campus have wireless access for use by alumni and guests. It is easy to connect to the MIT GUEST wireless network; your device should be able to "see" the network as one of the open wireless options and connect instantly.

 

Residence Hall Policies

Quiet Hours:

Quiet hours are in effect from 11:00 pm to 8:00 am daily, seven days a week, unless otherwise indicated by the building staff.

 

Behavior:

  • All persons using MIT residence halls shall adhere to all policies of MIT, as well as local, state, and federal laws concerning health, safety, and public order. All persons must have their Tech Reunions name badge with them at all times.
  • Destruction, vandalism, theft, and the movement of property owned or supplied by Housing & Residential Services is strictly prohibited, including but not limited to furniture and mattresses.
  • Playing of any sports activity in the hallways/common areas is prohibited.
  • Physical and sexual assault or verbal abuse, threats, intimidation, harassment, coercion, offensive language, or conduct which threatens or endangers the health or safety of oneself or another person, is prohibited.
  • Trespassing, tampering with, and/or unauthorized entry into Housing & Residential Services premises or property, including but not limited to roofs, elevators, offices, or balconies, is prohibited.
  • All individuals are expected to comply with the request of any MIT official (MIT Police, security staff, building staff, etc.) in performance of their duties.
  • No alcohol is permitted at gatherings in individual rooms or common areas in the residence halls.
  • Reminder, this is a living community at MIT. Please respect your neighbors and respect quiet hours.
  • Alumni and their registered guests can only access the residence halls they have been assigned.
  • The Tech Reunions Coordinator may immediately remove from housing, without refund, any person whose conduct is deemed unsafe or unacceptable to Housing and Residential Services. Reasons for immediate and permanent removal may include but are not limited to, use or possession of alcohol in common areas, public intoxication (regardless of age), use or possession of drugs and/or drug paraphernalia, tobacco, offensive language, health and safety violations, vandalism, and unauthorized entry to space. The Tech Reunions Coordinator, or their designee, in their sole discretion, has final authority regarding the sanction to be issued to an individual who commits a violation.

 

An Alumnus will be removed who:

  • Commits a serious violation of MIT's rules or policies, as defined by Housing & Residential Services
  • Engages in behavior that MIT deems unacceptable or warranting removal
  • Commits an infraction after already receiving a warning

 

Medical Issues:

MIT Medical does not provide emergency care.  For life-threatening situations or mental health emergencies, call 911 (or, if you are on campus, 617-253-1212).

Class Reunion Giving

How do I make a gift?

The easiest way to make a gift is with a credit card online at giving.mit.edu.  You can also learn more about other methods, including gifts by check, wire transfer, stock, mutual fund transfers, and donor-advised funds. 

What counts in my Class Reunion Gift total?

Gifts made in the four fiscal years leading up to the reunion year, excluding pledge payments previously counted in a reunion total

Gifts made in the 12-month period from July 1 through June 30 of the reunion year; and once a gift is made:

  • Multi-year pledges for which payments are due between July 1 and June 30 of the reunion year and final payment is due no later than June 30 of the fiscal year prior to the next reunion;
  • Recurring gifts prescheduled through June 30 of the fiscal year prior to the next reunion; and
  • Matching gifts received in the reunion year.

Class Participation will include donors who have made a gift in the 12-month period from July 1 through June 30 of the reunion year.  Gifts to all funds at MIT count towards Class Reunion Gift dollars and participation.

Tech Night at the Pops

What do I need to know about attending Tech Night at the Pops?

Dress code for the Pops is business casual or better. Those celebrating their 50th reunion and members of the Cardinal and Gray Society are encouraged to wear their red jackets. Symphony Hall does not allow children under the age of five to attend performances, and tickets are required for all patrons regardless of age. 

Tickets for Tech Night at Pops will be distributed digitally! Learn more about how to access your digital ticket here.

Symphony Hall is committed to creating a safe and healthy concert environment. All visitors must present either proof of full vaccination (two doses, or one where applicable) or proof of a negative test result to enter Symphony Hall. Mask wearing is strongly encouraged, but optional, while in Symphony Hall. Anyone who wishes to wear a mask is welcome to do so. These policies are subject to change as the COVID-19 pandemic evolves. Please check bostonpops.org/safety for the full details before your visit.
